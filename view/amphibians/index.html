Amphibians

Chinese Giant Salamander

Enter the worlds largest amphibian, the Chinese Giant Salamander. Sadly, they are also critically endangered due to the loss of their natural habitat... These things can grow up to 180cm and weigh about 25-30 kg (adult). Giant Salamanders have poor vision due to their tiny eyes but they have many sensory organs to compensate. Their diet consists of pretty much anything thats edible (fish, insects, mammals etc.) which gives you a range of options for feeding one. In addition, these amphibians have an extremely slow metabolism so they do not need to eat much at all.



Danube Crested Newt

The Danube Crested Newt is easily threatened by habitat loss and so, it is an endangered species. This little creature loves swampt and forest-like areas. They mainly eats worms which you should be able to buy from any pet store so keeping your Danube Crested Newt fed should be simple.



Strawberry Poison Dart Frog

The Strawberry Poison Dart Frog is the only Poison Dart Frog that is not endangered. They prefer rainforest-like environmnents and primarily feed on arthopods so it should be straightforward to maintain their diet as you can buy the standard worms from most pet stores. As these creatures are poisonous, we reccomend using gloves and protective clothing when physically approaching them.
